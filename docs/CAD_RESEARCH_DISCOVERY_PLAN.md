# CAD Research and Discovery Plan

This document is the working guide for exploring a possible CAD and public safety software opportunity without pulling focus away from the current TrueServe pilot. The goal is to create a structured research track, capture signal consistently, and avoid jumping into product development too early.

## 1) Purpose

The objective right now is not to build a CAD platform. The objective is to learn whether there is a real market gap, who feels the pain most, how strong the pain is, and whether there is a smaller entry point before considering a full platform.

This should stay in the **research and discovery** bucket until we can answer:

- Is the pain real and frequent?
- Who owns the problem?
- Who signs off on buying?
- How hard is it for agencies to switch?
- Is there a smaller wedge opportunity before full CAD replacement?

## 2) Priority rule

- **TrueServe pilot** remains the active build and launch priority.
- **CAD research** happens in parallel as a structured long-term exploration.
- No production CAD product work should begin until the research produces clear evidence of demand and a realistic entry point.

## 3) What we should build now

We should only build lightweight research infrastructure:

1. **Private research intake form**
   - Used to capture agency interviews, pain points, and follow-up notes
2. **Internal opportunity tracker**
   - Used to organize interviews, common themes, and possible entry products
3. **Problem-first concept page or one-pager**
   - Used only if needed for conversations, not as a public product launch
4. **Interview script**
   - Used to make sure every conversation captures comparable information

We should **not** build:

- live dispatch workflows
- incident routing logic
- CAD replacement tooling
- RMS modules
- 911 integrations
- operational public safety software

## 4) What we are trying to learn

The discovery effort should answer these questions:

- What systems are agencies using today?
- What do they dislike most about current vendors like Tyler or Hexagon?
- Who is affected most by the problems:
  - dispatchers
  - officers
  - supervisors
  - IT
  - procurement
- Are the problems about:
  - usability
  - reliability
  - reporting
  - integrations
  - support
  - cost
  - training
  - mobile access
- How painful is switching?
- Would they consider a smaller tool before a full replacement?

## 5) Research intake form

Use the following structure for every conversation or discovery lead.

### A. Contact and agency details

- Agency or department name
- City / state
- Contact name
- Contact role
- Contact email
- Contact phone
- Date of conversation
- Who on our team spoke with them

### B. Current environment

- Current CAD vendor
- Current RMS vendor if separate
- Years on current system
- Approximate size of department
- Who uses the system day to day
- Any known contract or renewal timing

### C. Pain points

- Top 3 pain points
- Most frustrating daily workflow issue
- How often the issue affects operations
- Whether the issue creates delays, confusion, errors, or extra manual work
- Whether the issue affects dispatch, field response, reporting, or management

### D. Switching readiness

- Have they considered switching vendors?
- If yes, why?
- If no, what stops them?
- Biggest switching barrier:
  - contract
  - cost
  - migration risk
  - training
  - integrations
  - procurement
- Would they consider a point solution instead of full replacement?

### E. Product opportunity signals

- What would they fix first if they could?
- Would they prefer:
  - better dispatch workflow
  - reporting / analytics
  - mobile responder support
  - QA / audit tooling
  - training / onboarding support
  - integration layer
- Would they talk again?
- Is this a potential pilot contact?
- Notes and direct quotes

## 7) Interview script

These questions should guide each conversation.

### Openers

- What CAD or dispatch system are you using today?
- How long have you been on it?
- What is your role in using or supporting that system?

### Pain discovery

- What do you dislike most about the current system?
- What slows people down the most?
- What frustrates dispatchers the most?
- What frustrates officers or field users the most?
- What do supervisors or administrators complain about most?
- Is the issue mainly usability, speed, reporting, mobile access, vendor support, or something else?

### Operational impact

- How often does this problem affect work?
- Does it create delays, rework, confusion, or manual workarounds?
- How hard is it to train new people on the current system?

### Buying and switching

- Have you considered switching vendors?
- What makes switching difficult?
- Who would influence or approve a change?
- Would you be open to a smaller tool that solves part of the problem without replacing the full system?

### Closing

- If you could fix one thing immediately, what would it be?
- Would you be open to a follow-up conversation if we wanted to validate ideas later?

## 8) What success looks like in discovery

This research track is successful if we can gather:

- 10 to 20 real conversations
- repeatable pain themes across multiple agencies
- clarity on the most painful workflows
- clarity on whether the wedge is replacement or support tooling
- at least a few agencies willing to continue the conversation

We do **not** need product code to decide whether this opportunity is real.

## 9) Likely wedge products to explore first

If the market is real, the best starting point is probably not a full CAD replacement.

More realistic first wedges:

- Dispatch workflow improvement layer
- Reporting and analytics module
- Mobile responder workflow support
- QA, audit, or compliance tooling
- Integration and data visibility layer

These are lower-risk discovery paths than trying to replace the full dispatch stack immediately.

## 10) Immediate next steps

1. Create the research intake form
2. Create the internal tracker
3. Use the interview script for every conversation
4. Capture at least 10 real agency discussions
5. Review the patterns before deciding whether to scope any product concept

## 11) Final recommendation

The right move is to stay disciplined:

- keep **TrueServe** as the active execution priority
- keep **CAD/public safety** in structured discovery mode
- build research infrastructure first
- let real conversations determine whether there is a viable opportunity

This keeps us from creating scope drift while still allowing us to explore a potentially valuable long-term product path.
